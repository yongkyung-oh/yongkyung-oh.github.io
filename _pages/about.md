### Biography

<div class="notice--primary" markdown="1">

Dr. YongKyung Oh is a Postdoctoral Researcher at the David Geffen School of Medicine, University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), contributing to the Medical & Imaging Informatics (MII) group. His work at UCLA Health builds upon his postdoctoral research at the Industry Intelligentization Institute at the Ulsan National Institute of Science and Technology (UNIST). His academic training at UNIST was interdisciplinary. He earned a Ph.D. in Industrial Engineering and also completed a Master's in Technology and Innovation Management and a B.Sc. in Physics. He was nominated as a Finalist for the UCLA Chancellor's Award for Postdoctoral Research (2025) in recognition of his recent achievements, including the CHIL 2025 Best Paper Award, an ICLR 2024 Spotlight paper, and multiple best paper finalist honors.

His research centers on developing trustworthy and robust AI methodologies to solve complex, real-world problems, with a primary focus on digital healthcare and precision medicine. While his foundational methodologies have proven effective across diverse sectors, including manufacturing, energy, maritime, and logistics, his recent work has increasingly focused on translating these advanced AI techniques to solve critical challenges in medical and healthcare. He possesses deep expertise in tailoring advanced AI techniques for specific domains and provides insightful perspectives on their practical, large-scale implementation.

In parallel with his academic pursuits, Dr. Oh has cultivated significant industry experience through visiting researcher positions at premier institutions such as Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) and the General Motors (GM) Global R&D Center. This experience underscores his ability to bridge innovative research with practical application.

</div>
